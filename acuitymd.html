Executive Summary

28
GEO Score. Critical

Brand has minimal intentional AIO presence. Citations are incidental, not authoritative. Core product positioning is absent from AIO entirely.

AcuityMD holds strong organic rankings and earns a Forbes startup recognition that surfaces in AI-generated responses. However, the brand's only AIO-cited page is a blog post about DRG coding, which causes AI systems to frame the brand as a billing reference tool rather than a MedTech intelligence platform.

Across 31 AIO-triggered queries, AcuityMD appears as a cited source in just 3, a 9.7% citation rate well below category norms for a software brand with this traffic profile. Key product areas including AcuityAI, Care Journeys, Contracts, Forecasting, and physician targeting are invisible to AI systems entirely.

The identity gap is the most urgent problem. AI systems currently place AcuityMD in three unrelated categories and attribute none of its core platform capabilities. Closing this gap requires deliberate content and credibility work, but the brand's existing organic footprint means progress can come quickly.

Three Findings That Matter

  • Misidentified in AIO. AI systems describe AcuityMD as a DRG coding resource and a generic medical sales CRM. The platform's actual positioning as a full-stack MedTech intelligence solution with real-world healthcare data and AI-powered insights does not appear in any observed AIO response.
  • Product pages earn zero AIO citations. The platform page, targeting, contracts, forecasting, and AcuityAI pages have no AIO presence whatsoever. Every citation the brand receives traces back to a single blog post on an off-topic subject.
  • External credibility signals are thin. Forbes provides one strong third-party mention, but no analyst coverage, software review domains, or peer-recognition sources appear in the AIO citation landscape alongside the brand.

Top Three Moves

  1. Build GEO-optimized product pages. Create structured, AI-readable content for the platform, AcuityAI, targeting, and contracts pages so product-level queries begin returning AcuityMD as a cited source rather than a blog reference.
  2. Expand the external credibility footprint. Pursue analyst coverage and software review domain listings to add third-party citation sources that reinforce product credibility inside AIO responses.
  3. Claim the solution-aware query layer. Develop focused content for high-intent queries such as best CRM for medical device companies and physician targeting software, where AIO triggers but AcuityMD is currently absent.

Off-Site Actions to Improve Citation Authority

  • Pursue software review platform listings. Structured profiles on peer-review and software directory sites give AI systems a credible, citable third-party source for product claims. These domain types are entirely absent from AcuityMD's current AIO citation set.
  • Activate the Forbes mention as a credibility anchor. The Forbes Next Billion-Dollar Startups citation already appears in AIO. Creating product content that references and links to this recognition can extend its citation halo to platform-level queries.
  • Earn analyst or research domain coverage. A single citation from a recognized health-technology research domain would introduce a new, high-authority domain type into the AIO source mix. Briefing independent analysts on AcuityAI and real-world data capabilities is the fastest path.
  • Develop co-citation opportunities with MedTech industry publications. AIO currently pulls from generic career and coding domains. Placing bylined content or data studies in MedTech trade publications would shift the domain mix toward category-relevant authority.
  • Encourage customer case study syndication. AcuityMD serves 400-plus MedTech companies. Customer-authored content published on external domains would introduce new citing pages outside the brand's own domain, diversifying the citation footprint.
  • Replace coding and career domain co-citations with commercial intelligence sources. The current peer domains (medical coding sites, nursing education pages) actively reinforce the wrong brand category. Displacing them requires building AIO-citable content that directly addresses physician targeting, market sizing, and MedTech forecasting topics.

1. Publish a GEO-Optimized Platform Overview Page

Rewrite it to explicitly define AcuityMD as a MedTech intelligence platform, name all modules (Care Journeys, Contracts, Forecasting, Markets, Targeting), and include structured entity definitions AIO can lift directly.

This single page addresses the identity gap where AIO currently describes the brand as a generic medical sales CRM instead of a full-stack commercial intelligence platform.

2. Create a Dedicated AcuityAI Feature Page

AcuityAI is a core product differentiator that is completely invisible in every observed AIO response. A standalone page with clear definitions, use cases, and outcome statements gives AIO a citable source for AI-related MedTech queries.

Target queries such as "AI for medical device sales" and "MedTech AI insights platform" where AIO triggers exist but no AcuityMD content currently competes.

3. Add GEO Markup to the Top Three Organically Ranking Blog Posts

Posts on medical device marketing strategy (position 3) and how to get into medical device sales (position 7) already rank well but receive zero AIO citations. Adding concise definition blocks, summary answer paragraphs, and explicit brand attribution to each post converts existing organic authority into AIO citation potential.

This is the fastest path to increasing the AIO citation rate from its current 9.7 percent because the rankings and traffic are already in place.

4. Secure One New Analyst or Peer-Review Domain Citation

The entire external citation landscape observed in this audit contains no analyst coverage and no software peer-review domain. A single listing or feature placement on a recognized software review destination would introduce a credibility-signal domain that AIO currently uses for competing products but not for AcuityMD.

Prioritize domains that already appear in AIO responses for "best medical device CRM" and "medical sales software" category queries, where AcuityMD needs stronger third-party reinforcement.

5. Publish a Physician Targeting and Territory Management Solution Page

Queries about physician targeting and territory management trigger AIO responses but AcuityMD is absent from all of them. A solution page combining both topics, with real-world data context and specific workflow descriptions, fills the single largest buyer-journey gap identified in this audit.

This page directly targets solution-aware buyers at the moment they are evaluating tools, which is the query layer where AIO citation drives the most qualified traffic.

Audit Method

Forty keywords were selected across three layers: brand-name queries, category queries, and alternatives queries. Each keyword was assessed for AIO trigger presence, brand citation presence, and citation page identity.

GEO Score components were weighted and scored against observed data, then combined into a single 0 to 100 index. The final score of 28 includes a minor upward adjustment for the Forbes recognition signal and the brand's strong organic traffic base.

Limitations

AIO responses are personalised and non-deterministic. The same query may produce different source lists across sessions, browsers, or geographies.

The 40-keyword set is a structured sample, not an exhaustive crawl. Low-volume or long-tail queries outside this set may produce different citation outcomes.

Traffic estimates from search-intelligence data are modelled figures. They reflect relative magnitude, not precise click counts.

Citation counts reflect observations at a single point in time. AIO inclusion can shift within days following content or algorithm changes.

What This Audit Does Not Do

This audit does not measure paid search performance, social media visibility, or direct referral traffic. It does not assess brand sentiment in news media or long-form editorial content beyond what appears in AIO source lists.

This audit does not constitute an SEO technical audit. Page speed, crawlability, structured data completeness, and backlink profile analysis are outside its scope. Those factors may influence GEO outcomes but are not scored here.

This audit does not track AIO performance on platforms other than Google Search. Emerging AI answer surfaces are not included in this scoring model.
