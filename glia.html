Executive summary

Glia holds strong organic rankings across financial services keywords yet appears in AI overview citations for only 3 of 40 tracked keywords. The brand's core product identity is being absorbed by a name collision with the neuroscience term "glia," which dominates 415 AI overview mentions and every top cited page.

Search-intelligence data shows that high-value category terms like "banking chatbot," "ai chatbot for banks," and "financial services call center" trigger AI overviews but do not include Glia. This means AI systems are answering buyer questions in Glia's market without surfacing the brand at all.

The opportunity is significant and addressable. Glia's top two commercial pages already draw meaningful organic traffic, providing a foundation to build GEO-optimized content that earns AI overview citations in the financial services contact center category.

GEO Score 18 / 100 AI overview inclusion rate. 3 inclusions out of 40 tracked keywords, with 0 inclusions on high-volume commercial category terms

Three findings that matter

  • Name collision is catastrophic for AI visibility. Every AI overview triggered by the keyword "glia" cites neuroscience sources. Glia the company earns no citation on its own brand name query, which carries 5,500 monthly searches.
  • Commercial category queries are AIO-active but Glia is absent. Twelve high-value keywords including "ai chatbot for banks" (250 volume) and "banking chatbot" (500 volume) trigger AI overviews where Glia is not cited despite holding organic positions 2 through 10.
  • Organic traffic is concentrated in too few pages. The homepage and two product pages account for the vast majority of traffic, leaving the brand exposed if those rankings shift and providing almost no GEO surface area across the category.

Top three moves

  1. Publish a definitive, structured "what is Glia" brand disambiguation page that clearly separates the company from the neuroscience term, using entity-clear language AI systems can cite directly.
  2. Rewrite the top two commercial pages to include direct, quotable answers to the category questions that currently trigger AI overviews without Glia, targeting inclusion in those 12 high-value overview slots.
  3. Build at least six net-new editorial pages targeting category keywords where AI overviews fire, such as "video banking," "virtual branch," and "co-browsing software for banking," to expand the brand's citeable surface area for AI systems.

Framing Issues

The brand name "Glia" is a common neuroscience term, meaning AI systems default to biology content when the bare brand name is queried. Of 415 total search-intelligence data mentions detected, every cited page references biological glial cells, with zero citations pointing to glia.com.

All 20 top cited pages in AI overviews belong to medical and academic domains such as NIH, Wikipedia, and Nature. Glia the company receives no identity reinforcement from any AI overview triggered by its own brand name at scale.

The three queries where glia.com does appear in AI overviews are "glia," "salemove," and "digital branch," each in a limited or peripheral role. For the highest-volume branded query ("glia" at 5,500 monthly searches), the AIO describes biology exclusively and the company page ranks only organically in position 1 beneath it.

This framing gap is a structural risk. Any buyer or evaluator querying the brand name in an AI-powered search tool will receive biology definitions, not a vendor description, unless they use more specific product-level terms.

30-Day Priority Moves

1. Create a Dedicated "What Is Glia" Brand-Answer Page

Search-intelligence data shows "what is glia" triggers an AI Overview yet glia.com earns no citation there. A concise, definition-first page on the /about URL can supply AI engines with a quotable, structured answer about Glia the fintech brand.

The page should open with a single declarative sentence identifying Glia as a banking AI platform, followed by a brief product summary. This format matches the answer patterns AI engines pull from cited sources.

2. Publish an AI Overview-Optimized Page for "AI Chatbot for Banks"

The keyword "ai chatbot for banks" carries 250 monthly searches and triggers an AI Overview, but glia.com is absent from that overview despite holding position 2 organically. Adding a structured FAQ block and a direct definitional lead paragraph to the existing /ai-chatbot-banking page closes this gap.

Each FAQ answer should be self-contained and under 50 words. That format is consistent with the snippet lengths AI engines prefer when assembling overview citations.

3. Build a Comparison Landing Page Targeting Alternatives Queries

Prompts such as "Glia alternatives" and "Glia vs competitors" are active in the audit prompt set, yet no comparison-focused page exists on glia.com. A neutral, fact-based comparison page positions Glia as the authoritative source on its own competitive landscape.

The page should include a simple feature-comparison table covering key differentiators. AI engines frequently cite brand-owned comparison content when no independent review dominates the overview.

4. Add Structured Data and Category Signals to the /ai-call-center-financial-institutions Page

This page ranks second for "financial services call center" (250 searches) and second for "bank call center software" (70 searches), both of which trigger AI Overviews where Glia is uncited. Adding SoftwareApplication schema and a bolded category definition at the top of the page increases AI engine parseability.

Category signals such as "financial services contact center platform" should appear in the first 100 words. AI engines weight early, explicit categorization when selecting sources for overview citations.

5. Resolve the Neuroscience Name-Collision with Targeted Content Signals

The 415 AI Overview mentions tracked in search-intelligence data belong almost entirely to neuroscience content about glial cells, not to Glia the company. Every uncited AI Overview for branded queries like "what is glia" defaults to biology sources because glia.com lacks explicit commercial-context signals.

Adding a short "About Glia the Company" schema block with Organization markup, plus consistent use of the phrase "Glia banking AI platform" in page titles and meta descriptions, helps AI engines disambiguate the brand name within 30 days.

Method

Search-intelligence data was queried for AI Overview trigger rates, citation counts, and organic keyword positions across 40 tracked keywords. Each prompt was assigned to a layer and evaluated for brand presence or absence in AI-generated responses.

AI Overview inclusion was recorded when glia.com appeared as a cited source within a triggered overview. Absence was treated as a data point indicating a citation gap, not a ranking failure.

Limitations

Search-intelligence estimates reflect modeled traffic and volume figures, not verified click data. Actual visits may differ from reported estimates.

AI Overview composition changes frequently. Findings represent a snapshot at the audit date and may not reflect current AI responses.

Only 40 keywords were tracked in this audit. Category coverage is illustrative, not exhaustive, across the full financial services AI landscape.

The brand name "Glia" is shared with a neuroscience term. All 415 search-intelligence mentions and cited pages in the dataset reflect biological glia, not the Glia technology company. Brand-specific AI Overview inclusion was effectively zero.
