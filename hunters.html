THREE FINDINGS THAT MATTER
01

The brand is missing from most of AI-answer queries

AIO triggers on 30% of tracked keywords, which is a reasonable baseline trigger rate for this category. However, the triggers are almost all on non-cybersecurity 'hunters' queries, not SIEM or SOC queries. Hunters is cited in only 1 of 40 tracked queries, a citation rate of 2.5%. Zero hunters.ai pages appear in the cited pages list. The single AIO citation is likely incidental, not category-driven. All 20 top cited pages in the AIO data belong to unrelated domains. None are hunters.ai pages. Top cited page is a PubMed article about HIV.

02

AI misreads the category

Critical. The entity collision between the common noun 'hunters' and the brand 'Hunters.ai' is severe. AIO has no established identity for Hunters as a cybersecurity brand. The brand self-identifies as a next-gen SIEM for small SOC teams, but AIO does not reflect this identity at all. This is not a factual error but a fundamental entity disambiguation failure at the platform level.
