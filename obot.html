THREE FINDINGS THAT MATTER
01

The brand is missing from most of AI-answer queries

Obot ranks first in AIO for its own brand name but the response is heavy with disambiguation prompts competing with TV and healthcare homonyms. No AIO triggers exist for core category queries like 'mcp security', 'ai agent governance', or 'enterprise mcp hosting'. Triggered queries are misaligned with business positioning, pulling from blog content about cloud storage, RAG, and prompt engineering.

02

AI misreads the category

AIO treats Obot as a generic open-source gateway rather than an enterprise AI governance platform. Homonym competition (TV personality, healthcare acronym) forces disambiguation, which dilutes the brand positioning. AIO does not reliably place Obot in any category-level queries related to enterprise AI security or MCP hosting.
